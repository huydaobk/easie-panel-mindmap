# EASIE SANDWICH PANEL SYSTEM ## A. Fundamentals & System Overview ### A.1 What is a Sandwich Panel? - Composite: 2 thin faces + 1 thick core - I-beam analogy: faces resist tension/compression, core resists shear - Up to 90% weight savings vs conventional steel ### A.2 Panel Types by Application - Wall panels (vertical load-bearing) - Roof panels (spanning, weatherproof) - Cladding panels (non-structural envelope) - Partitions / ceilings (interior) - Structural / frameless panels (load-bearing shell) ### A.3 Functional Layers - Face sheets (steel, aluminum, FRP) - Core (PUR, PIR, mineral wool, EPS) - Adhesive/bond layer - Joints & interfaces ### A.4 Performance Categories - Mechanical (strength, stiffness) - Thermal (insulation, U-value) - Airtightness / Watertightness - Fire (reaction & resistance) - Acoustic - Durability ## B. Materials & Component Properties ### B.1 Face Materials - Steel facings (280-320 MPa yield) - Coatings: galvanized, Aluzinc, HPS200, color-coated - Surface geometry: flat, ribbed, profiled, micro-rib ### B.2 Core Materials - **PUR/PIR Foam**: density 34-42 kg/m³, shear 0.04-0.14 MPa - **Mineral Wool (MW)**: density 100-125 kg/m³, shear 0.08 MPa, fire-resistant - **EPS**: economical, moisture-sensitive - **PVC/IPN (Divinycell H)**: high performance/weight ratio - **PET (Divinycell P)**: excellent thermal stability - **PES (Divinycell F)**: highest service temperature, fire/smoke performance ### B.3 Material Properties - Shear modulus & shear strength - Tensile/compressive strength - Wrinkling-related parameters - Creep & long-term behaviour - Thermal conductivity (lambda) ### B.4 Bond & Interface Behaviour - Adhesive types and curing - Bond quality indicators - Debonding failure modes ## C. Standards, Design Basis & Validation ### C.1 EN 14509:2013 Framework - Factory-made self-supporting panels - CE Marking requirements - Factory Production Control (FPC) ### C.2 European Recommendations - ECCS-CIB Joint Committee guidance - Design-by-Calculation (DBC) - Design-by-Testing (DBT) ### C.3 Safety Philosophy - Partial factors (gamma_M, gamma_G, gamma_Q) - Reliability levels - Reference period ### C.4 Validation Routes - **DBC**: analytical calculation with declared properties - **DBT**: testing with statistical evaluation (k=0.85 for 1 test, 0.90 for 2 tests) - Parametric study: DBT vs DBC comparison ### C.5 Supporting Standards - EN 10143 (tolerances) - EN 10346 (coated steel) - EN 13162 (mineral wool) ## D. Structural Behaviour & Failure Mechanisms ### D.1 Load Introduction & Load Paths - Point loads vs distributed loads - Support conditions: simple, continuous, cantilever - Load transfer through joints ### D.2 Global Panel Behaviour - Bending: face stresses, core shear - Shear deformation contribution - Composite action effectiveness - Deflection: bending + shear components ### D.3 Stability & Second-Order Effects - Global buckling - Local instability - P-Delta effects in frameless systems ### D.4 Connection Stiffness Influence - Joint stiffness on global behaviour - Semi-rigid modelling - Force distribution ### D.5 Failure Modes - **Face yielding**: plastic collapse - **Wrinkling**: local buckling of compressed face - **Core shear failure**: shear cracking/yielding - **Tensile failure in core**: rare but critical - **Debonding/delamination**: adhesive failure - **Fastener/joint failures**: pull-through, shear - **Local crushing**: support effects, point loads ### D.6 Limit States - **ULS**: Ultimate (strength, stability) - **SLS**: Serviceability (deflection L/200, vibration) - **FLS**: Fatigue (if applicable) ## E. Joints, Fasteners & Interface Detailing ### E.1 Joint Types - Longitudinal joints (side laps) - End joints (transverse) - Corner joints - Support connections ### E.2 Fastener Design - Screw types and embedment - Pull-out resistance - Shear resistance - Thermal expansion accommodation ### E.3 Interface Performance - Thermal bridging minimization - Air leakage control - Water penetration resistance - Vapor diffusion ### E.4 Joint Stiffness & Load Transfer - Stiff vs flexible joints - Load redistribution - Compatibility of deformations ### E.5 Detailing Rules - Minimum edge distances - Fastener spacing - Sealant requirements - Flashing details ## F. Openings, Penetrations & Discontinuities ### F.1 Types of Openings - Small: width < panel width, within single panel - Large: width ≥ panel width, spanning joints - Service penetrations (ducts, pipes, cables) - Windows and doors ### F.2 Design Principles - Local load redistribution - Force paths around openings - Reinforcement concepts ### F.3 Unreinforced Small Openings - Reduction factors: kF (wrinkling), kC (shear) - Beta = b/B ratio - Limitations and applicability ### F.4 Reinforced Openings - Adapter profiles (load-bearing) - Punctual fixings (load distribution) - Bonded frames (stabilization only, ≥5cm zone) - Framed openings with structural steel ### F.5 Special Cases - Profiled panels: flat zone vs rib zone reduction - Clustered openings - Edge and corner effects ### F.6 Verification - Wrinkling check - Shear check - Joint capacity check ## G. Worked Examples, Design Aids & Workflow ### L.1 Worked Examples by Application - **Wall examples**: wind pressure, self-weight - **Roof examples**: snow load, uplift, deflection - **Cladding examples**: wind suction, thermal - **Frameless examples**: structural shell design - **Opening/detail examples**: reinforced cut-outs ### L.2 Key Formulas (Integrated) - Bending resistance: M_Rd = f_yf * Z / gamma_M - Shear resistance: V_Rd = f_cv * A_c / gamma_M - Deflection: delta = delta_bending + delta_shear - Wrinkling stress: sigma_w = k_w * E_c^(1/3) * E_f^(2/3) * (t_f / b_c) ### L.3 Design Workflow 1. Define application and design actions 2. Choose panel system and materials 3. Select validation route (DBC or DBT) 4. Check structural behaviour (bending, shear, stability) 5. Detail joints and resolve openings 6. Verify durability, fire, serviceability 7. Final detailing and QA checklist ### L.4 Design Checklist - [ ] Actions defined (permanent, variable, wind, snow, thermal) - [ ] Panel type and core material selected - [ ] Validation route confirmed (DBC/DBT) - [ ] ULS checks: bending, shear, compression, wrinkling - [ ] SLS checks: deflection, vibration - [ ] Joint capacity verified - [ ] Openings detailed and checked - [ ] Fire requirements met - [ ] Durability considered (ageing, environment) - [ ] Manufacturing quality assured - [ ] Installation requirements specified ## M.

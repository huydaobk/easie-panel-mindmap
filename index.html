# EASIE PANEL - SANDWICH PANEL KNOWLEDGE MINDMAP ## 1. SANDWICH PANEL BASICS ### 1.1 What is a Sandwich Panel? - Composite: 2 thin faces + 1 thick core bonded together - Analogous to I-beam: faces carry tension/compression, core carries shear - Up to 90% weight savings vs conventional steel ### 1.2 Sandwich Principle - Lightweight core separates materials → dramatically increases stiffness/strength - Environmental benefit: lighter structures = less energy over 25 years ### 1.3 Three Essential Components - Faces: Steel (280-320 MPa), stainless, aluminum, FRP - Core: PU, PUR, PVC, PET, PES, balsa - supports skins, prevents buckling - Adhesive: Bonds faces to core - de-bonding = catastrophic failure ### 1.4 Structural Behavior - Bending: Compression upper face, tension lower face, shear in core - Shear: Core resists sliding between faces - Compression: Core supports faces against wrinkling ## 2. CORE MATERIALS ### 2.1 PU / PUR Foam - Density: 34-42 daN/m³ - Shear: 0.04-0.14 MPa | Compression: 0.1 MPa - Thicknesses: 60, 80, 100, 130, 140, 200mm ### 2.2 Mineral Wool (MW) - Density: 100-125 daN/m³ | Shear: 0.080 MPa - Fire resistant but moisture sensitive ### 2.3 PVC/IPN Foam - Divinycell H series - good performance/weight ### 2.4 PET Foam - Divinycell P - excellent thermal stability ### 2.5 PES Foam - Divinycell F - highest service temperature, fire resistant ### 2.6 Core Comparison - PU: lightweight, versatile - MW: fire resistant - PVC/PET/PES: higher performance ## 3. STANDARDS & TESTING (EN 14509) ### 3.1 BS EN 14509:2013 - Factory-made sandwich panels for wall and roof cladding - CE Marking requirements ### 3.2 Key Test Methods - Full-Scale Bending: Q_el, Q_c, failure modes - Large Shear Tests: 3m specimen - Small-Scale: tensile, compression, shear, creep ### 3.3 Design by Testing (DBT) - Step 1: Get mechanical properties - Step 2: Statistical evaluation (k=0.85 for 1 test, 0.90 for 2) - Step 3: Calculate stresses and deflections - Step 4: Superposition of load cases - Step 5: Identify decisive limit state ## 4. DESIGN METHODS ### 4.1 Limit State Design - ULS: Failure prevention - SLS: Deflection L/200 ### 4.2 Bending Capacity - M_Rd = f_yf * Z / gamma_M - Face yielding and wrinkling checks ### 4.3 Shear Capacity - V_Rd = f_cv * A_c / gamma_M ### 4.4 Deflection - delta_total = delta_bending + delta_shear ## 5. FIRE SAFETY & DURABILITY ### 6.1 Fire Performance - PES: excellent fire/smoke properties - MW: inherently fire-resistant ### 6.2 Durability Tests - DUR1: 75C, RH<15% (dry) - DUR2: 65C, RH>95% (humid) ### 6.3 Ageing Results - PU: stabilizes after initial drop - MW: humidity causes degradation - 30-year panels still meet requirements ## 7. MANUFACTURING & QC ### 7.1 EN 14509 Requirements - Controlled manufacturing - Quality management system ### 7.2 Quality Parameters - Face: material, thickness, coating - Core: density, bond quality - Geometry: tolerances ### 7.3 Handling & Installation - Lift to prevent bending - Proper joint sealing - Fastener spacing ## 8. OPENINGS (EASIE WP1 - D1.2) ### 8.1 Classification - Small: width < panel, no joint cut - Large: width = or > panel ### 8.2 Small Openings - Reduction factors kF (wrinkling), kC (shear) - Based on beta = b/B ### 8.3 Load Transfer - Via longitudinal joints to adjacent panels - Delta_M less than 2*M_joint: no frame needed ### 8.4 Profiled Panels - Flat part: 3-6% decrease - Profiled part: 20-35% decrease ### 8.5 Reinforcement Types - Adapter profiles: carry load - Punctual fixings: distribute load - Bonded frames: STABILIZE only (>= 5cm zone) ### 8.6 Design Checks - Wrinkling | Shear | Joint failure ## 9.
